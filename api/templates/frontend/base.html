Biorhythms are based on the idea that a person's life is on a cycle, with peaks and troughs. Using mathematical formulas, people can calculate and graph their cycles, thus determining good days (peaks) and bad days (troughs). Biorhythms can be broken down into three cycles: the physical, the emotional, and the intellectual. Some even go further and look at the spiritual, aesthetic, and intuitional cycles (although we will only focus on the main three). The physical cycle follows a 23-day period and focuses on stamina, health, and strength. The emotional cycle follows a 28-day period and is related to creativity, emotions, and intuition. Finally, the intellectual cycle lasts 33 days and is associated with thinking, judgment, and concentration.
